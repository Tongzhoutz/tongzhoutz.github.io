Papers

Theses

  • Nonlinear Earnings Dynamics: an Extended Panel Data Framework with an Application in the PSID Data
    • Ph.D Thesis

    Tong Zhou

    TLDR
    We extend a new quantile-based panel data framework to study the nature of income persistence. Log-earnings are the sum of a persistent component and a transitory component, both following two general hidden Markovian processes. We establish the nonparametric identiﬁcation of the double nonlinear earnings processes. Conducting the income data in recent waves of the Panel Study of Income Dynamics, we ﬁnd that the earnings process features nonlinear persistence and conditional skewness. We also ﬁnd the transitory component is also endowed with fair nonlinearity, rejecting the hypothesis that it is an independent innovation. We then show that the impact of earnings shocks varies substantially across earnings histories.

  • Measurement Errors in Stochastic Frontier Models without using auxiliary data

    • Master Thesis

    Tong Zhou

    TLDR
    We propose an estimation strategy for correcting measurement errors in linear stochastic frontier models. A stochastic frontier model is featured with a composite error, posing a challenge for handling measurement errors in the panel data setting. The proposed estimation strategy exploits higher moments of existing regressors without resorting to instruments outside the model. We also establish its asymptotic normality. Our simulation result shows the proposed estimation strategy works well.

Research in Progress

  • Dimension-Free Density-based Clustering methods in High-dimensional Data with random knots
    • Authors

    Tong Zhou

    TLDR
    We propose a method to overcome the curse of dimensionality and to apply density-based clustering to high-dimensional data, when protoclusters (knots) are generated from data. We first find a large set of knots using a density-based criterion that is estimable even in high-dimensions. These similarity measures behave like the bones connecting knows. Finally, we merge knots according to a linkage criterion to create the final clusters.
  • The evolution of partially identified set of nonparametric identification with many binary instruments
    • Authors (alphabetical)
    Tong Zhou, Yingyao Hu
    TLDR
    We show that the partially identified set of a nonparametric identification problem is not shrinking as the number of available binary instruments keeps increasing. This counter-intuitive result suggests that more binary instruments cannot help reveal any more information on the function(s) of our interests, unless full identification is achieved. For applied practitioners, it suggests that there is no free lunch to use binary instruments.
  • Flyclient: Super-Light Clients for Cryptocurrencies
    • Authors

    B. Bünz, L. Kiffer , L. Luu and M. Zamani

    Paper (Published at IEEE S&P (Oakland) 2020)
    Talk at Zcon
    Slides
    TLDR
    We present Flyclient which is a protocol that lets a super-light client determine which is the correct (longest) proof of work chain. The client only needs to download a logarithmic number of block headers (say 200 instead of 1 million). The protocol is a non-interactive proof of proof of work (NiPoPoW) that shows that a chain has at least x amount of work put into it. It uses a random sampling of block headers to ensure that an adversary with limited mining power could not have produced this chain.
  • Batching Techniques for Accumulators with Applications to IOPs and Stateless Blockchains
    • Authors (alphabetical)

    D. Boneh, B. Bünz and B. Fisch

    Paper (Published at Crypto 2019)
    Talk at Scaling Bitcoin 18
    Slides Technical Slides
    TLDR
    Accumulators are short commitment to a set that support efficient inclusion and exclusion proofs. We present several new batching techniques for accumulators and positional vector commitments in group of unknown order. Our techniques can be used in a stateless blockchain design where all users and miners only require a constant amount of storage. We also present a new vector commitment which can significantly reduce the proof size of IOP instantiations, such as STARKs.
